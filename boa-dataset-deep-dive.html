BOA is the legally-binding regulatory gazette of the Government of Aragón. For us it is the second Group-1 pipeline source after BOE, chosen because Aragón is the team's Forestalia/Maestrazgo competitive territory. The critical thing to internalise: BOA does not contain "projects." It contains administrative acts about projects. Everything below follows from that.

Provenance legend used throughout: live-verified confirmed against the source or a primary regulatory text this week · spike from our Sprint 0 spike (one real PDF + synthesised examples) · domain Spanish-permitting domain knowledge, not yet checked against a live BOA renewable doc.

1 · What BOA is

live-verified The Boletín Oficial de Aragón (boa.aragon.es) is the official journal of the Comunidad Autónoma de Aragón, published by the Departamento de Presidencia. It carries the legally-binding text of regional laws, decrees, orders, administrative resoluciones, environmental-impact decisions (DIAs), and public-consultation openings. A document has no legal effect until it appears here — publication is the act of notification.

Structurally it mirrors the BOE: five Roman-numeral sections, each grouped by DEPARTAMENTO DE … headings. For renewables only two sections and two departments matter:

SectionContainsRenewable relevance
I. Disposiciones GeneralesLaws, decrees, orders of general applicationRare — framework changes only
II. Autoridades y personalHR / appointmentsNone
III. Otras disposiciones y acuerdosResolutions, administrative authorisationsHigh — AAP/AAC grants & DIAs land here
IV. Administración de JusticiaCourt noticesNone
V. AnunciosPublic-consultation openings, EIA info-pública, procurementHigh — PC openings & info-pública live here

The two departments that issue renewable acts:

  • Departamento de Industria, Competitividad y Desarrollo Empresarial — the órgano sustantivo (substantive body). Grants the administrative authorisations (AAP/AAC) and opens info-pública on access requests. In practice acts are signed by the Servicio Provincial of Zaragoza / Huesca / Teruel.
  • INAGA — Instituto Aragonés de Gestión Ambiental (under the Departamento de Agricultura, Ganadería y Medio Ambiente) — the órgano ambiental. Opens environmental info-pública and issues the DIA. live-verified Real INAGA DIAs for hybrid PV+wind projects ("Entreviso", "Rané") appear in BOA in 2024–2025.

2 · How the dataset is generated each day

BOA is produced by an editorial/publication chain, not an API. The daily lifecycle:

Dept. submits act→ Editorial typesetting
Adobe InDesign→ Assembled bulletin
sections I–V, CSV codes→ Published as PDFs
via BRSCGI→ Legally in force
  • live-verified Cadence: daily Monday–Friday, plus urgent extraordinarios that carry their own numbering. Each issue has a number (e.g. Número 186, 25 Sep 2025) and a year ("AÑO XL").
  • spike Each document gets a CSV code (Código de Verificación Segura) of the form BOA{YYYY}{MM}{DD}{NN} — date plus a within-day serial. This is deterministic and is what we use as source_document.external_id.
  • spike Bodies are PDFs (BOE is XML). Produced server-side by Adobe InDesign, served via BRSCGI?CMD=VEROBJ&MLKOB=…&type=pdf. Byte-stable once published; no URL rotation. We extract text with PyMuPDF.
  • spike There is no clean list API. The SPARQL endpoint on opendata.aragon.es is stale since 2025-01-23; the CKAN dataset exposes no per-day URL; the only operational interface is the Angular SPA calendar. So the loader must discover each day's document IDs by scraping the SPA's date-scoped results page (MLKOB IDs are internal DB IDs, not derivable from the date).
The key mental model A day of BOA = a bundle of independently-authored administrative acts, glued into one numbered PDF bulletin by an editorial process. We reverse that: split the bulletin back into per-act documents, classify each, and extract the renewable-relevant ones into typed events.

3 · Who creates the "projects" in the gazette

domain Nobody does — and this is the most important conceptual point in this whole document. A gazette entry is an act of an administration about an expediente. The "project" as we model it is a reconstruction we build by linking many acts that share a project name + applicant + cadastral references. The chain of actors:

ActorRoleWhat they produce in BOA
Promotor / titular
(the developer)		Files the application (solicitud) for a specific installation. Creates the underlying project — but off-gazette, as a private filing.Nothing directly. They are named in others' acts.
Órgano sustantivo
(Dept. de Industria → Servicio Provincial)		Processes the authorisation; opens info-pública on the access request; grants AAP & AAC.RESOLUCIÓN (Sec. III) and ANUNCIO de información pública (Sec. V)
Órgano ambiental
(INAGA, Aragón)		Runs the environmental assessment; opens EIA info-pública; formulates the DIA.ANUNCIO de información pública (Sec. V) and RESOLUCIÓN/DIA (Sec. III)
Gestor de red
(REE transmission / Endesa e-distribución)		Grants the access & connection permit (permiso de acceso y conexión) at a substation.Mostly not in BOA — lives in REE/Endesa capacity tables (our Group 2). The grid node is the join key.
Implication for our pipeline The developer "creates" the project by filing; the administrations then emit a stream of dated acts about it over 2–5 years. Our project entity is a rollup; the project_linker stitches a project name like "Parque Eólico Sierra del Pobo II" across its PC opening, its DIA, and its AAP/AAC grant — events that may sit months apart and be signed by different bodies.

4 · The permit lifecycle & what each status means

live-verified A Spanish renewable project clears a defined sequence of permits. The order matters because each one is a separate BOA event, and RDL 23/2020 attaches a statutory deadline (hito) to each — miss one and the grid access permit expires and the economic guarantee is executed.

How a status is encoded in BOA

spike The (section × department) cross-reference tells us the event type without reading the body:

Dept. de INDUSTRIAINAGA / Medio Ambiente
Sec. III (resoluciones)AAP / AAC granted → RESOLUTION_GRANTEDDIA → DIA_PUBLISHED
Sec. V (anuncios)Notice / info-pública on access → ANUNCIO_PROVIDEDEIA info-pública opens → PC_OPENED

A DIA additionally carries an outcome: FAVORABLE, DESFAVORABLE, or FAVORABLE CONDICIONADA. We split this into permit_event.event_outcome — a desfavorable DIA is a project-killer and a direct trigger for our predicted-capacity-emergence model.

5 · Does the status (AAP/AAC/DIA) reflect all developers who applied?

No. A BOA status is always single-expediente, single-promoter. Each announcement resolves one project promoted by one developer. There is no such thing in BOA as a node-level or "all-applicants" status.

domain Why this matters, concretely. Several developers can hold or seek capacity at the same substation — that is the normal competitive situation, and where capacity is scarce it becomes a formal concurso (contest). Each applicant runs an independent expediente that progresses through the milestones on its own clock. So at a single node you might simultaneously have:

  • Developer A — DIA favorable obtained, AAP granted (milestone 3 cleared)
  • Developer B — still in información pública (milestone 1)
  • Developer C — desistimiento / DIA desfavorable (dropped out)

BOA reports each of these as a separate dated act. It never emits a combined "status of substation X." That node-level competitive picture is something we synthesise — it does not exist in any single source.

This is exactly why our data model anchors on substation + access_date. We attach every per-expediente event (permit_event → substation_mention) to the grid node, then read across them to assemble the node view BOA never gives you: "who is queued at this substation, at what milestone, with how much MW." The capacity-contention read and the predicted-emergence view both depend on this reassembly. A contest_reservation flag marks nodes under formal concurso, which gates the prediction (reserved capacity does not freely re-emerge).

Corollary: when you see "AAC granted" in BOA, read it as "this developer's expediente reached milestone 4," not "this substation is now built out." Two developers at the same node can both be mid-pipeline; one getting AAC says nothing about the other's standing or about residual free capacity — for that you need the REE/Endesa capacity snapshot (Group 2).

6 · Why BOA at all — regional vs national competence

live-verified Spain splits permitting authority by installed capacity and geography:

Regional competence → BOAState competence → BOE
Threshold≤ 50 MW, within one autonomous community> 50 MW, or spanning >1 CCAA, or offshore
Substantive bodyDept. de Industria, Gobierno de AragónDG Política Energética y Minas (MITECO)
Environmental body (DIA)INAGADG Calidad y Evaluación Ambiental (MITECO)
Dossier warehouseAragón portal (aragon.es)MITECO portals → almacen.redsara.es

So BOA and BOE are not redundant — they cover different projects partitioned by the 50 MW line. A 250 MW PV plant is BOE+MITECO; a 49.5 MW plant (a very common deliberately-sized figure) is BOA+INAGA. This is also why dossier capture has two source paths (§7).

7 · When are the dossiers created (and by whom)?

The "dossier" is the full technical file — Memoria, Estudio de Impacto Ambiental, SHP boundary, Separatas de afectados, EM-fields and SyS studies (~100–300 MB). Your question "when does MITECO create the dossiers" has a precise answer:

The dossier is published at the moment información pública opens — i.e. simultaneously with the PC_OPENED event. It is not created at application time (that filing is private), nor at DIA/AAP time. It is posted so the public can review the project and file alegaciones during the consultation window (typically 30 days). domain

So the dossier-creation event and our PC_OPENED event are the same moment. Two source paths depending on competence:

  • National (>50 MW) → MITECO. spike The BOE anuncio embeds a per-CCAA MITECO portal URL (mptmd.gob.es/portal/delegaciones_gobierno/…). That portal links to a per-shipment warehouse URL almacen.redsara.es/sending/public/{uuid} holding the files. The warehouse page states a reception date and a validity-until date.
  • Regional (≤50 MW) → Aragón. live-verified The BOA anuncio points to the Aragón catalogue "Proyectos en información pública", filterable by infrastructure type, open/closed claim period, and province.
The operational pain point — and why we capture it. Dossiers live for ~60 days from the reception date (the public-consultation window), then disappear. spike Nothing IBV uses today tracks that expiry. Our Phase 1 plan: capture the pointer at PC_OPENED (T1.6), probe the manifest daily (T2.6), surface an "expiring this week" panel (T4.7), and one-click-fetch the archive into Azure Blob before it vanishes (T3.5). Real worked example in the deck: PFot-ALM-151 Los Arcos Hybridisation — reception 08/05/2026, validity 03/07/2026, ~233 MB across 5 files.

Phase 1 runs the manifest probe for Aragón only (where ccaa = 'aragon'); non-Aragón MITECO URLs are still captured opportunistically but not probed. Full 17-CCAA coverage and any dossier content extraction (Memoria / SHP / Separatas) are deferred to Phase 2.

8 · Technical access shape (how we ingest it)

Document body spike

Stable PDF fetch:

GET …/BRSCGI?CMD=VEROBJ
    &MLKOB={13-digit id}&type=pdf
→ 200, application/pdf, %PDF-1.7

MLKOB is an internal DB id — not derivable from the date. PyMuPDF extracts ~32K chars cleanly across 8 pages.

Daily discovery spike

No list API. Scrape the SPA date page:

…/#/resultados-fecha
   ?PUBL-C={YYYYMMDD}
   &SECC-C=Disposiciones+o+Anuncios
   &NUMB-C={normal|extraordinario}

Open question Q1: Playwright headless vs an undocumented RSS feed vs emailing the publisher for a daily JSON.

Canonical citation spike

http://www.boa.aragon.es/eli/es-ar/
   {type}/{YYYY}/{MM}/{DD}/{num}/dof

ELI URI — stable, use as source_document.url. Types: d decreto, o orden, dl decreto-ley, r resolución. Resolves to the SPA, not a fetchable doc.

Posture spike

  • robots.txt lists ~600 specific blocked VEROBJ ids ("derecho al olvido"); honour it via RobotsGuard. Current-day docs aren't on it.
  • Rate limit: 2.0 s floor (legacy CGI). ~5–30 PDFs/day → ~1 min/day.
  • User-Agent: needs a Mozilla-style UA or the endpoint resets the connection.
  • Charset: BRSCGI HTML is ISO-8859-1; decode as Latin-1. PDF text is UTF-8.
  • Licence: CC BY 4.0 — commercial reuse OK with attribution.

10 · How a BOA document maps to our schema

BOA daily PDF bulletin
  └─ split per administrative act ─┐
                                   ▼
  source_document                  (one row per act)
    source_id      = 'boa'
    external_id    = CSV code  BOA{YYYYMMDD}{NN}
    url            = ELI URI
    published_at   = parsed from PDF 'subject'
    body           = PyMuPDF text
    metadata       = {section, departamento, mlkob, gazette_number, gazette_type, eli_uri}
        │
        ▼ extractor (boa-v1 prompt — PDF-aware, NOT BOE-v4)
  permit_event                     (the dated regulatory act)
    event_type     ∈ {PC_OPENED, RESOLUTION_GRANTED, DIA_PUBLISHED, ANUNCIO_PROVIDED}
    event_outcome  ∈ {pendiente, aprobada, denegada, desistida}   ← DIA fav/desf lives here
    project_id     → resolved later by project_linker (name + applicant + cadastral)
    developer_id   → resolved later by developer_linker (CIF + name)
        ├─ permit_milestone   (hitos 1–5 from RDL 23/2020, with due_date)
        ├─ substation_mention (one per cited node+kV → substation master, REE-canonical names)
        └─ project_documentation (if anuncio embeds a MITECO/Aragón dossier pointer)

No BOA-specific schema change is needed — BOA's event taxonomy is a subset of BOE's. The only BOA-specific engineering is the PDF→text path and a dedicated boa-v1 extractor prompt (BOE-v4 is an XML/text-parser and won't transfer to flattened multi-column PDF text without a de-hyphenate / page-glue preprocessing step).
