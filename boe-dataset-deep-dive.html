BOE is the legally-binding national gazette of the Kingdom of Spain. For Phase 1 it is Group 1 pipeline source #1 (BOA is #2): the canonical regulatory-events channel for every >50 MW renewable project under state competence. The mental model is identical to BOA's — BOE does not contain "projects"; it contains administrative acts about projects — but the technical access shape, the cadence, and the issuing administrations are very different.

1 · What BOE is

live-verified The Boletín Oficial del Estado (boe.es) is the official gazette of the Spanish state, published by the Agencia Estatal Boletín Oficial del Estado under the Ministry of the Presidency. It carries the legally-binding text of organic laws, royal decrees, ministerial orders, administrative resoluciones, environmental-impact decisions (DIAs), and public-consultation openings. A document has no legal effect until it appears here — publication is the act of notification.

Five Roman-numeral sections, each grouped by DEPARTAMENTO DE … headings. For renewables only two sections and one ministry matter:

SectionContainsRenewable relevance
I. Disposiciones generalesLaws, RDs, RDLs, normative ordersRare — framework changes only (RDL 23/2020, RDL 7/2025…)
II. Autoridades y personalAppointments, oposicionesNone
III. Otras disposicionesAdministrative resolutions, grants, authorizationsHigh — DIA, AAP, AAC, DUP grants land here (BOE-A-{y}-{seq})
IV. Administración de JusticiaCourt notices, edictosNone
V. AnunciosPublic-consultation openings, info-pública, procurementHigh — info-pública anuncios live in V-B (BOE-B-{y}-{seq})

The one ministry that issues renewable acts:

  • Ministerio para la Transición Ecológica y el Reto Demográfico (MITECO) — department code 9575 in the BOE XML. live-verified Confirmed verbatim against the XML of BOE-A-2025-6776. Two Direcciones Generales do nearly all the renewable lifting:
    • DG Política Energética y Minas (DGPEM) — the órgano sustantivo. Issues AAP, AAC, DUP, AEX. Signs the resoluciones in Section III.
    • DG Calidad y Evaluación Ambiental (DGCEA) — the órgano ambiental. Formulates the DIA (also in Section III).
  • For projects in a single province the AAP info-pública anuncio may also be signed by an Área de Industria y Energía in the Delegación / Subdelegación del Gobierno (Section V-B) — acting por delegación of the DGPEM. This is the "BOE-B prefix" surface.

2 · How the dataset is generated each day

BOE is, structurally, what BOA tried to be: a publication chain with a real machine interface bolted on. The daily lifecycle:

Body submits act→ Editorial typesetting→ Daily edition assembled
sections I–V, BOE-A / BOE-B serials→ Published
XML + JSON sumario + PDF→ Legally in force
  • live-verified Cadence: "El 'Boletín Oficial del Estado' se publica todos los días del año, salvo los domingos." — BOE Open Data FAQ. That's 6 days a week including most holidays, not Mon–Fri as one might assume by analogy with BOA. Occasional suplementos use their own pagination but share the day's edition number.
  • live-verified Each document gets a stable identifier of the form BOE-A-{year}-{seq} (Sections I–IV) or BOE-B-{year}-{seq} (Section V Anuncios). The seq is a year-global running serial within each prefix, not per section and not per ministry. We use it as source_document.external_id.
  • live-verified Bodies are XML-first. The canonical structured form is at www.boe.es/diario_boe/xml.php?id={identifier}. PDF and HTML variants exist; the PDF is the legally-authentic copy, the XML is the cleanest input for our extractor.
  • live-verified There is a clean list API. The Open Data programme exposes www.boe.es/datosabiertos/api/boe/sumario/{YYYYMMDD}, with content negotiation between XML (default) and JSON via Accept header. This is the gift BOA does not give us — no SPA scraping required.
The key mental model A day of BOE = a tree of independently-authored administrative acts, glued together by an editorial process and exposed via a published JSON/XML index. We hit the sumario for date D, walk sección → departamento → epígrafe → item, filter on a relevance regex (department name + title keywords), then fetch each survivor's XML body and run the boe-v4 extractor on it.

Implication for our pipeline The developer "creates" the project by filing; MITECO then emits a stream of dated acts about it over 2–5 years. Our project entity is a rollup; the project_linker stitches a project name like "Planta solar fotovoltaica 'Los Vientos I'" across its información pública, its DIA from DGCEA, and its AAP from DGPEM — events that may sit months apart and be signed by different DGs. The BOE-v4 extractor populates project_name, developer_name + developer_tax_id, and cadastral refs to make this join robust.

4 · The permit lifecycle & what each status means

live-verified A Spanish renewable project under state competence (>50 MW or multi-CCAA or offshore) clears a defined sequence of permits. Each one is a separate BOE event, and RDL 23/2020 (BOE-A-2020-6621) attaches statutory deadlines (hitos) indexed against the date the access permit was obtained — miss one and the grid-access permit lapses and the economic guarantees (≈ 40,000 €/MW under RD 1183/2020) are executed.

0Access & connection permitPermiso de acceso y conexiónREE / distributorstart of clock(Group 2)
1Application admitted & info-pública opensInformación pública (AAP + EsIA, often bundled)DGPEM via Delegación6 months (admisión a trámite)PC_OPENED

4Construction authorisationAutorización Administrativa de Construcción (AAC)MITECO DGPEM28 mo (→ 37 mo post-RDL 29/2021)RESOLUTION_GRANTED

The deadlines above are the 2018-2020 cohort counted from RDL 23/2020's entry into force (25 June 2020); later cohorts use different reference dates. domain RDL 7/2025 (BOE-A-2025-12857) exempted generation and storage installations ≤500 kW from needing AAP/AAC — irrelevant at utility scale, but it means small projects vanish from BOE entirely.

Outcome dimension

Each lifecycle act additionally carries an outcome: FAVORABLE / DESFAVORABLE / FAVORABLE CONDICIONADA (DIAs) or otorgada / desestimada / denegada / desistida (resoluciones). After the 2026-05-19 IBV reconciliation we split this into permit_event.event_outcome — a desfavorable DIA or a desestimación is a project-killer and a direct trigger for our predicted-capacity-emergence model. live Real example of a kill: BOE-A-2024-26100 (desestimación of V Solar 1 / Shell, 246,23 MW).

5 · Does the status (AAP/AAC/DIA) reflect all developers who applied?

No — same as BOA. A BOE status is always single-expediente, single-promoter. Each announcement resolves one project promoted by one developer. There is no such thing in BOE as a node-level or "all-applicants" status.

domain The mechanics are slightly different at the state level than at the autonomic level. At transmission nodes (the >50 MW band that BOE covers), formal concursos are more common because grid capacity scarcity is sharper, and RDL 23/2020 imposes the same caducidad clock on every queued expediente. So you can have, at one REE substation simultaneously:

  • Developer A — AAP granted (BOE-A from DGPEM, hito 3 cleared)
  • Developer B — DIA favorable but AAP not yet (BOE-A from DGCEA only, hito 2)
  • Developer C — desistimiento or desestimación (BOE-A from DGPEM, terminal)
  • Developer D — still in información pública (BOE-B from Delegación, hito 1)

BOE reports each of these as a separate dated act. It never emits a combined "status of substation X." That node-level competitive picture is something we synthesise — it does not exist in any single source.

This is exactly why our data model anchors on substation + access_date. We attach every per-expediente event (permit_event → substation_mention) to the grid node, then read across them to assemble the node view BOE never gives you. A contest_reservation flag marks nodes under formal concurso, which gates the prediction (reserved capacity does not freely re-emerge).

BOE-specific subtlety: the extractor distinguishes grid-connection substations (REE/DSO-owned, the join key) from developer-built substations (project-specific elevadora). Both can appear in a single BOE anuncio body (spike see boe_extractor.py:118-134); only the former joins CNMC capacity tables.

6 · Why BOE and not BOA — state vs regional competence

live-verified The competence rule is Article 3.13 of Ley 24/2013 del Sector Eléctrico (BOE-A-2013-13645). The state (MITECO via DGPEM) is competent if any one of these triggers is met:

  • Letter (a) — 50 MW threshold: "Instalaciones peninsulares de producción de energía eléctrica, incluyendo sus infraestructuras de evacuación, de potencia eléctrica instalada superior a 50 MW"
  • Letter (b) — multi-CCAA: the project (including evacuation line) crosses an autonomous- community boundary
  • Letter (c) — offshore: located in territorial sea

Otherwise competence falls to the relevant CCAA. So BOE and BOA are partitioned, not redundant:

State competence → BOERegional competence → BOA (Aragón slice)
Threshold>50 MW peninsular, or multi-CCAA, or offshore≤50 MW within one CCAA
Substantive bodyMITECO DGPEMDept. de Industria, Gobierno de Aragón
Environmental body (DIA)MITECO DGCEAINAGA
Dossier warehousessweb.seap.minhap.es/almacen/… liveAragón portal (aragon.es)

A 250 MW PV plant is BOE+MITECO; a 49.5 MW plant (a very common deliberately-sized figure) is BOA+INAGA. The two sources cover different projects, which is also why dossier capture has two source paths (§7).

7 · When are the dossiers created (and where they actually live)?

Same precise answer as BOA: the dossier (Memoria, Estudio de Impacto Ambiental, SHP boundary, Separatas de afectados, EM-fields and SyS studies, ~100–300 MB) is published at the moment información pública opens — i.e. simultaneously with our PC_OPENED event so the public can review the project and file alegaciones.

Live-verified — three URL patterns coexist in 2026 A May 2026 probe of seven BOE-B anuncios shows the warehouse URL pattern segments by issuing Subdelegación, not by year. Three patterns are simultaneously in use:
  1. https://almacen.redsara.es/sending/public/{uuid-v4} — modern GEISER/ORVE warehouse. live BOE-B-2026-15230 (Lleida) — also the only body that printed an explicit expiry line: "Los ficheros permanecerán disponibles para su descarga hasta el 24/07/2026" (73 days from publication).
  2. https://ssweb.seap.minhap.es/almacen/descarga/envio/{40-hex} — legacy SEAP warehouse, still in active use. live BOE-B-2026-4036 (Valencia, BESS Valle Solar); BOE-B-2024-27073 (Córdoba, Carbo).
  3. No direct file URL. Body points only to the per-CCAA index at mptmd.gob.es/portal/delegaciones_gobierno/delegaciones/{ccaa}/proyectos-ci/… or, in Andalucía, to an acortar.link shortener. The scraper must traverse one click deeper to reach the dossier ZIP. live Alicante (BOE-B-2026-15059), Cuenca (BOE-B-2026-14190), Sevilla (BOE-B-2026-12664).
T1.6 must handle all three patterns plus shortener-resolution. docs/requirements/miteco-project-documentation-synthesis.md has been updated 2026-05-28 with the same finding.

Información-pública duration

  • live-verified For the AAP request: "20 días hábiles" (RD 1955/2000 art. 125 + art. 144 for DUP).
  • live-verified For the EIA package: "un plazo no inferior a treinta días hábiles" (Ley 21/2013 art. 36 — BOE-A-2013-12913). When the trámite is conjunto (Art. 14 Ley 21/2013) the 30-day EIA window dominates.
  • live-verified Recently extended: RDL 7/2026 (BOE-A-2026-6544) extended the standard renewable información-pública to 45 days. Anuncios post-March-2026 should use the 45-day window.
The operational pain point — and why we capture it. The warehouse links are ephemeral. live Only one of seven 2026 anuncios printed an explicit expiry, and that one was 73 days — so treat the IBV deck's "~60 day" figure as a planning approximation, not a measured constant. T2.6 should set validity_until only when the body actually states it; null otherwise. Our Phase 1 plan: capture the pointer at PC_OPENED (T1.6 — extends the BOE-v4 extractor), probe the manifest daily (T2.6), surface an "expiring this week" panel (T4.7), and one-click-fetch the archive into Azure Blob before it vanishes (T3.5).

Phase 1 runs the manifest probe for Aragón-scoped projects only (per docs/spikes/sprint-0/post-meeting-decisions.md); non-Aragón MITECO URLs captured by the BOE extractor are still stored opportunistically but not probed. Full 17-CCAA coverage and any dossier content extraction (Memoria / SHP / Separatas) are deferred to Phase 2.

spike XML is byte-stable. Phase-0 client uses 2.0 s min interval (stricter than sumario, because xml.php is the legacy endpoint).

ELI URIs live

https://www.boe.es/eli/es/
   {type}/{YYYY}/{MM}/{DD}/{num}/{version}

Stable canonical citation for numbered legislación only (leyes, RDs, RDLs). Important caveat: resoluciones in Sec. III and anuncios in Sec. V do not carry ELI URIs. Address those by their BOE-A-… / BOE-B-… identifier instead. ELI dimensions: jurisdiction, type, year, month, day, number, version (dof original / con consolidated), language, format.

Posture live / spike

  • robots.txt: www.boe.es disallows verifica.* endpoints, search-result pages, and hundreds of individual blocked document IDs ("derecho al olvido"). No Crawl-delay directive found. Honour it via RobotsGuard.
  • Rate limit: not published; community convention ≈ 1 req/s. Phase-0 enforces 1.0 s on sumario, 2.0 s on xml.php.
  • 5xx retry: 3 attempts, exponential backoff (Phase-0 tenacity config).
  • Licence: Spanish PSI reuse regime — attribution required ("Fuente de los datos: Agencia Estatal Boletín Oficial del Estado"), no misrepresentation. Not CC BY in name (despite the common shorthand).
  • User-Agent: identify yourself; default Python UAs sometimes get throttled.
  • Charset: all responses UTF-8.

10 · How a BOE document maps to our schema

BOE daily edition
  ├─ sumario tree walked (sección → departamento → epígrafe → item)
  ├─ relevance filter (departamento regex + title keywords)
  └─ per surviving item, fetch xml.php?id={identifier}
                                   ▼
  source_document                  (one row per act)
    source_id      = 'boe'
    external_id    = BOE-A-{Y}-{seq}   or   BOE-B-{Y}-{seq}
    url            = txt.php URL  (canonical for non-ELI items)
    published_at   = metadatos.fecha_publicacion
    body           = <texto> UTF-8 string
    metadata       = {section, departamento_codigo, departamento, eli?, pdf_url}
        │
        ▼ extractor  (boe-v4 prompt — XML-aware; ships in phase-0)
  permit_event                     (the dated regulatory act)
    event_type     ∈ {PC_OPENED, RESOLUTION_GRANTED, DIA_PUBLISHED, ANUNCIO_PROVIDED, …}
    event_outcome  ∈ {pendiente, aprobada, denegada, desistida}   ← DIA fav/desf lives here
    project_id     → resolved by project_linker (name + applicant + cadastral)
    developer_id   → resolved by developer_linker (CIF + name verbatim)
        ├─ permit_milestone   (hitos 1–5 from RDL 23/2020, with due_date)
        ├─ substation_mention (kind ∈ {grid_connection, developer_built}; only grid_connection
        │                       joins CNMC capacity tables)
        └─ project_documentation (if anuncio embeds ssweb.seap.minhap.es/almacen/… → T1.6 pointer)

Already operational: BOE loader at archive/phase-0/ingestion/boe/ and extractor at archive/phase-0/extraction/boe_extractor.py (MODEL_VERSION = "boe-v4", SCHEMA_VERSION = "boe-v5").
